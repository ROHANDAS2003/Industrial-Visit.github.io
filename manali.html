Four Day Manali Industrial Tour Itinerary

Day 1, Pick up at Chandigarh/Delhi by our representative , Industrial Visit at First Company Delhi/Chandigarh/Baddi as per the pick up , Place , Lunch then Industrial Visit to second Company (Pharma, FMCG, Mechanical)followed by Dinner and Night Journey to Manali.
Day 2. Reach Manali by morning, transfer to your pre-booked hotel. After checking in, take a short break and take a stroll through the beautiful city of Manali. Post lunch , half day sight seeing, visit the mystical Hadimba Devi Temple, which locals call the heart of Manali and nestled among the beautiful Deodar trees. Then go to Manu Temple, Bashshit Temple to feel the spiritual atmosphere. Surrounded by shops selling handicrafts and carpets, the Tibetan Monastery is a famous must-visit landmark. In the evening, shop and stroll along bustling Mall Road. Return to hotel for dinner. Overnight in Manali.

Day 3, Wake up early in the morning, enjoy a hearty breakfast, and head to the lush, misty high mountains of Rotang Pass or Solang Valley . The Rohtang Pass/Solang Valley allows you to engage in adventure activities such as skiing, snowmobiling or snowmobiling for an additional fee. On the way to Rotang Pass, you will pass the small town of Kochi, the Gulava Lookout, La Halla Falls and the Maki Slums with roadside restaurants. This is the perfect photo zone where you can take bright poses and capture rich beauty. Scenic drive through the high pass before returning to your hotel for a delicious dinner. Overnight in Manali. Day 4, Early wake up in the morning, right after breakfast , Check out and you start your journey to the beautiful city of Kulu, which is called “The Beautiful Valley of the Gods”. See huge and majestic snow-capped hills and a sparkling river running nearby. After spending time in the beautiful Kullu Valley, enjoy white water rafting , followed by Journey back to Delhi or Chandigarh. WHERE SKY MEETS MOUNTAINS

Situated at the edge of the valley, Manali is one of the most attractive tourist destinations not only in Himachal Pradesh, but also in the world. Manali is synonymous with the magnificence of streams and birdsong, forests and gardens and snow-capped mountains. Manali is the true starting point of the ancient trade route through Rotang and Baralacha through Lahul and Ladakh to Kashmir, with a branch road leading to Spiti. Road connections are now provided to Leh in Jammu and Kashmir, the Pangi Valley in Chamba, and Kazi in Rahul and Spiti. In summer there is a regular bus from Manali to this place. It is located 45 km from Kullu.

There is an interesting legend in Manali that, after the Great Flood, Manu , the author of Manu Samhita, first set foot on a place on earth in a boat in the sky. The specific place where he established his abode was present-day Manali, and is believed to have been renamed Manulaya, Manu’s abode. A temple dedicated to Manu still exists in the village of Manali.

A Paradise for adventure sports enthusiasts, Manali is located 6,398 feet above sea level in the Himalayas. Located in the Kulu region of Himachal Pradesh, the snow-capped peaks are a popular destination for honeymooners. Considered the twin city of Kullu, Manali’s scenic landscape, history and culture give it the nickname Valley of the Gods.

Manali became very popular in the European and other western Countries in the 60’s and 70’s when hippies flocked to the city in search of their utopian dreams. Food and room rates nearly triple during Christmas and New Years, the summer months with the most tourists. Buses from Manali, the main departure point for Ladakh, Lahaul and Spiti, run daily from June to October to Leh, Keylong and Kaza.
